# MARKET MAP

## Purpose

Compare the first three target specialties to decide where to start and what should remain shared versus specialty-specific.

## Summary Table

| Specialty | Market shape | Typical practice type | Operational complexity | Price sensitivity | Compliance and insurance burden | Best use as target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acupuncture | Mid-sized, fragmented niche | Solo or small clinic | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate | Best first wedge |
| Massage therapy | Large but highly fragmented | Solo, rented room, mobile, spa-adjacent | Low to moderate | High | Low | Best second specialty |
| Chiropractic | Larger clinics and more structured operations | Solo and group practice | High | Moderate | High | Later expansion only |

## Acupuncture

### Practice Characteristics

- Many solo or small clinics
- Cash-pay common, with some mixed insurance participation
- Visits often repeat over a course of treatment
- Herbs, formulas, and wellness-oriented follow-up may matter

### Current Software Pattern

- Specialty tools such as `Unified Practice` and `AcuSimple`
- Broader clinic platforms such as `Jane`
- Some clinics combine schedulers, form tools, and payment systems instead of using one platform

### Market Advantages

- Strong founder credibility and domain familiarity
- Good fit for treatment plans, packages, and follow-up automation
- Specialty workflows are meaningful but still manageable

### Market Challenges

- Smaller total market than massage
- Some practices are cost-conscious
- Mixed expectations around insurance support

### Common Pain Points

- Charting is too rigid or too slow
- Notes and treatment plans do not flow well together
- Patient communication and retention are weak
- Herbs and related inventory are often awkward

### Fit for Initial Launch

Strong. Best balance of founder-market fit, manageable complexity, and meaningful need for specialty depth.

## Massage Therapy

### Practice Characteristics

- Very large and fragmented market
- High percentage of self-employed practitioners
- Many part-time, mobile, rented-room, or small studio practices
- More self-pay and membership/package models

### Current Software Pattern

- Booking-led products such as `MassageBook`, `ClinicSense`, `Vagaro`
- Generic business stacks such as `Square` plus a scheduler and forms

### Market Advantages

- Simpler clinical complexity
- Strong need for booking, reminders, packages, memberships, and retention
- Fast path to a polished operational product if core workflows are already built

### Market Challenges

- Higher price sensitivity
- Lower switching urgency if current scheduler is “good enough”
- Larger share of buyers may resist advanced or premium software

### Common Pain Points

- Generic booking tools do not understand bodywork workflow well
- Reporting is weak
- Packages, discounts, and memberships are messy
- Mobile and room-flow experience can be awkward

### Fit for Initial Launch

Good for expansion after the core is proven. Less ideal than acupuncture for the first wedge because pricing pressure is stronger.

## Chiropractic

### Practice Characteristics

- Solo and group offices
- Heavier documentation expectations
- More recurring visit plans
- Insurance, PI, and referral workflows are more common

### Current Software Pattern

- Chiropractic-focused PM/EHR products such as `ChiroTouch`, `ChiroFusion`, `Genesis`
- Larger all-in-one office management systems with billing depth

### Market Advantages

- Higher willingness to pay
- Strong pain around documentation and office operations
- Opportunity for high-value workflow improvement

### Market Challenges

- Insurance complexity
- Higher support expectations
- Harder migration requirements
- More compliance and billing risk

### Common Pain Points

- Documentation burden is heavy
- Billing workflows are painful
- Legacy systems are difficult to use and support
- Reporting often does not answer day-to-day operational questions

### Fit for Initial Launch

Poor as a first target for a solo founder seeking low stress. Better as a later expansion once the platform and support model are stable.

## Shared Workflow Potential

The following are strongly shared across all three specialties:

- lead intake and referral capture
- patient registration and intake
- consent forms
- scheduling and reminders
- treatment documentation
- checkout and payments
- rebooking and retention
- basic business reporting

This means a single core platform is realistic.

## Specialty Extension Potential

Acupuncture needs:
- TCM intake
- point charting
- herb and formula workflows

Massage therapy needs:
- body-region tracking
- modalities
- outcall and tips

Chiropractic needs:
- exam templates
- care plans
- heavier billing and insurance workflows

This means specialty-specific modules should remain extensions, not forks.

## Recommended Entry Order

1. Acupuncture
2. Massage therapy
3. Chiropractic

Rationale:
- Starts where founder insight is strongest
- Reuses the core for massage with relatively low added complexity
- Defers the highest-stress billing and compliance burden
