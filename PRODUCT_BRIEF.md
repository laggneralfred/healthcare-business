# PRODUCT BRIEF

## Working Name

Alternative and complementary care practice-management platform

## Vision

Create a modular, calm, AI-assisted practice platform for small alternative healthcare clinics that replaces fragmented admin work with a cleaner day-to-day operating system.

The product should feel more practical than generic scheduling tools and less bloated than legacy specialty software.

## Initial Target User

Primary target:
- Solo and small acupuncture clinics in the United States

Initial buyer profile:
- Owner-operator or small clinic owner
- Cash-pay or light-insurance mix
- High sensitivity to admin burden
- Needs scheduling, notes, checkout, and patient retention more than enterprise compliance machinery

Secondary future users:
- Massage therapy practices
- Chiropractic clinics

## Core User Problems

- Too many disconnected tools for intake, scheduling, notes, payments, and follow-up
- Specialty software often feels clunky, slow, or overbuilt
- Generic tools are easier to use but miss important clinical workflows
- Rebooking and retention are handled inconsistently
- Owners lack simple, actionable business insight
- Documentation takes time that should go to patient care

## Product Promise

Give small complementary healthcare practices one operational core for running the business, plus specialty modules that fit their actual clinical workflow, with AI that saves time inside real tasks.

## Why This Can Win

- Strong shared workflow across specialties supports a modular architecture
- Specialty-specific layers can be added without rebuilding the business core
- Practical AI can create real differentiation in charting, intake review, and retention
- Founder domain knowledge reduces discovery risk in acupuncture

## Differentiation

Compared with generic scheduling tools:
- Better clinical workflow support
- Better treatment-note flow
- Better patient lifecycle and rebooking support

Compared with niche legacy software:
- Simpler operating model
- More modern workflow design
- Modular expansion across specialties
- More useful AI with lower friction

Compared with broad healthcare software:
- Lower complexity
- Better fit for small clinics
- Less operational overload

## Product Shape

Shared core:
- CRM and referral tracking
- Patient records
- Intake and consent
- Scheduling
- Encounter notes
- Checkout, payments, packages
- Messaging and reminders
- Reporting

Specialty extensions:
- Acupuncture
- Massage therapy
- Chiropractic

AI layer:
- Intake summarization
- Draft note generation
- After-visit summary drafts
- Rebooking and retention prompts
- Later, chart QA and business copilot

## Platform Direction

Initial platform assumption:
- Build on self-hosted `Odoo Community`

Reason:
- Low starting cost
- Strong operational foundation
- Good modularity
- Suitable for iterative custom development

Constraint:
- The product must remain disciplined enough that Odoo customization does not become the business.

## Business Approach

Go narrow first:
- One niche
- One clear workflow spine
- One manageable onboarding path

Do not optimize for largest market first.
Optimize for lowest-stress path to a useful, defensible product.

## Initial Success Criteria

- Practitioners can run daily scheduling, intake, notes, and checkout in one system
- AI meaningfully reduces charting and follow-up effort
- Buyers describe the product as calmer and easier than current tools
- Early customers stay engaged because the product saves time every week
